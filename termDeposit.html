Data Description

The dataset includes bank client data such as age, job, marital status, education, credit default, balance, housing loan, and personal loan. It also includes information about the last contact of the current campaign, including contact communication type, last contact day of the month, last contact month of the year, and last contact duration. In addition, it includes other attributes such as the number of contacts performed during this campaign, the number of days that passed by after the client was last contacted from a previous campaign, the number of contacts performed before this campaign, and the outcome of the previous marketing campaign. The output variable is whether or not the client has purchased a term deposit.



Data preparation

Before using the data for training, it is important to perform data preparation steps, which include checking for missing values, handling them appropriately (e.g., imputing or removing), and checking for unique values to ensure that there are no duplicates or irrelevant data that may affect the performance of the machine learning models. Additionally, other preprocessing steps such as feature scaling, encoding categorical variables, and splitting the data into training and testing sets should also be performed to ensure that the data is properly formatted and ready for machine learning modeling. By performing these data preparation steps, the resulting machine learning models are more likely to be accurate and reliable.

Model Evaluation

In the given scenario, the model evaluation metric used is the AUC (Area Under the Curve) score, and the best model is the XGB Classifier with an AUC score of 0.924. In this case, an AUC score of 0.924 indicates that the XGB Classifier model performs well in distinguishing between positive and negative classes, with a high level of accuracy. However, the AUC score may not provide a complete picture of the model's performance, and other metrics should be considered, such as accuracy, precision, recall and f1 score.
